🌱 Program Overview

Our youth training program is built around real work, real responsibility, and real mentorship. We don’t teach from a classroom — we teach from the field, where youth learn hands-on skills that build confidence, discipline, and purpose.

Every youth in our program learns:

  • Tree climbing fundamentals and professional safety practices
  • Tool handling, rope management, and situational awareness
  • Environmental stewardship and respect for nature
  • Teamwork, leadership, communication, and accountability

🌳 Professional Tree Climbing & Safety

Our youth learn professional tree climbing from experienced mentors who work in the field every day. This is not recreational climbing — this is real arborist training, taught with industry‑standard gear and safety protocols.

Youth learn how to:

  • Use harnesses, saddles, helmets, and ropes correctly
  • Perform safe ascents and controlled descents
  • Inspect trees for health, hazards, and structural weaknesses
  • Move confidently and safely in elevated environments
  • Respect the environment while working in it

This training builds strength, focus, discipline, and trust — skills that carry into every part of life.

📘 Business & Job Skills Training

We don’t just train youth to climb — we train them to build a future. Our program includes real business education so youth can turn their skills into opportunities.

Youth learn how to:

  • Find clients and understand what homeowners look for
  • Give estimates and communicate pricing clearly
  • Land jobs through professionalism and confidence
  • Stay organized with scheduling, tools, and responsibilities
  • Practice proper etiquette when speaking with clients
  • Build a reputation based on reliability and respect

These business skills prepare youth not just for tree work — but for any career path they choose.

🌿 Community Projects

Training is tied directly to service. Youth don’t just practice skills — they use them to restore and uplift the community.

  • Cleanup days in neglected areas
  • Tree trimming and hazard removal
  • Light building and repair projects
  • Beautification and restoration efforts

Every project teaches responsibility, teamwork, and pride in making a real difference.

Parents, guardians, or youth themselves can reach out to start the enrollment process.
