Our Mentors

Our Story: A Brotherhood of Tree Professionals

Our mentorship team is built from a tight-knit group of professional arborists, climbers, and community workers who have known each other for over a decade. We’ve climbed together, worked storms together, cleaned neighborhoods together, and built careers side-by-side.

One day we realized something: we’ve been blessed with skills that can change lives.

So we made a decision — to teach the community, especially the youth, the art and discipline of being an arborist.

This program is our way of giving back, passing down real skills, and building the next generation of leaders.

What Mentors Do

Our mentors are more than instructors — they are protectors, guides, and examples.

  • Teach safe tree climbing and rope systems
  • Train youth in tool handling and ground operations
  • Lead real community service projects
  • Model discipline, teamwork, and leadership
  • Help youth build confidence and purpose
How Mentorship Works

The Boardwalk Clay Mentorship Path

Our mentorship program is structured, safe, and built for real growth.

1. Orientation & Safety

Youth learn the basics: PPE, knots, communication, ground safety, and respect for tools.

2. Ground Operations

Before climbing, youth master raking, hauling, rope management, and teamwork.

3. Beginner Climbing

Under full supervision, youth learn basic ascent, positioning, and controlled movement.

4. Intermediate Skills

Rigging, limb control, saw safety (non-powered), and environmental awareness.

5. Leadership & Service

Youth lead small teams during community cleanups and service projects.

6. Certification Path (Optional)

We help youth pursue real arborist certifications and job opportunities.
