🧹 Neighborhood Cleanup Day

Date: Every 2nd Saturday

Location: Rotating neighborhoods

Our cleanup days are more than just picking up trash — they are **community gatherings** where neighbors, youth, mentors, and volunteers come together to restore pride and safety in overlooked areas.

These events often include:

  • Trash removal, brush clearing, and beautification
  • Cookouts and community meals after the work is done
  • Networking between families, youth, and local leaders
  • Live demonstrations on knots, rope handling, and safety basics
  • Mentors teaching youth how to work as a team in the field

Cleanup days teach youth responsibility, leadership, and the importance of giving back — while strengthening the community around them.

🌳 Tree Work Volunteer Day

Date: Monthly

Location: Announced weekly

Tree Work Day is where youth get hands-on experience with **real arborist skills** under mentor supervision. Volunteers help with ground support, cleanup, and environmental care.

These events include:

  • Tree trimming and hazard removal
  • Climbing demonstrations by trained youth and mentors
  • Lessons on knots, rigging, and rope safety
  • Discussions on environmental stewardship and tree health
  • Cookouts and community bonding after the work is complete

Youth learn discipline, focus, and confidence — and volunteers get to be part of their growth.

🌱 Youth Training Workshop

Date: Twice per month

Location: Boardwalk Clay Training Grounds

Our workshops are **full training days** where youth learn professional tree climbing, safety, teamwork, and leadership. These are structured sessions designed to build real skills and real confidence.

Workshops include:

  • Climbing practice with harnesses, ropes, and helmets
  • Knot-tying lessons and rope management drills
  • Team-building exercises and leadership challenges
  • Business lessons: finding clients, giving estimates, professionalism
  • Networking with mentors, volunteers, and community partners

These workshops prepare youth not just for tree work — but for **life**, teaching discipline, communication, and how to carry themselves professionally.
